"Obi Wan has taught you well" — Lord Vader.

In professional life, when asked where we learned skills, responses typically include "something from college" or "picked it up myself." Rarely can we name the specific person who taught us.

**The Star Wars Model**

Padawans receive complete education — emotional mastery, impulse control, moral judgment, etiquette fundamentals. Only after mastering basics do they train under Masters who provide real-world experience. Similarly: martial arts demand master training; hacking requires guru mentorship; family businesses employ apprenticeship under relatives. Many business empires operate through generations without formal business training — yet hire MBAs.

**The Problem**

Thousands of students annually migrate through institutions, chanting "I must get a good school/college" without understanding why, what, or from whom they learn. Institutions exploit this, charging enormous fees for mere names and stamps. Few institutions advertise faculty by name — only "qualified and dedicated faculty."

Problems:
- Mass testing; admission based on payment ability
- No personal teacher interviews
- Teachers have no say selecting students
- Teaching is poorly rewarded
- Communication protocol mismatches between teaching and learning styles

**Indian Tradition**

Indians traditionally viewed education as seekers journeying to find mentors, not institutions. Gurudakshina differs fundamentally from fees: given after course completion when mutual satisfaction exists, proportional to knowledge imparted. Gurudakshinas ranged from Ekalavya's thumb to Arjun's war victory.

**A Proposal**

Informal associations practicing mentorship:

- No prescribed syllabus or defined fees
- Members at different hierarchy levels based on peer analysis
- Each member serves as mentor, apprentice, or both
- Initial mentors advertise skills they can impart
- Apprentices apply to masters offering desired learning
- Masters select worthy applicants
- Teaching occurs in the mentor's preferred style
- Apprentices select masters matching their learning styles

Students gain satisfaction learning from experienced mentors. Mentors learn to organize thoughts, present ideas, and recognize their knowledge's worth. Relationships require proportional demands — apprentices may reject demands if mentors haven't earned respect.

The methodology could elaborate indefinitely. What requires consideration: enterprise feasibility and locating initial mentors.

Here I leave the topic open for feedback.
