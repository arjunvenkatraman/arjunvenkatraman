A dialogue between two internal voices — Alter 1 and Alter 2.

**Alter 1** opens with the observation that isolation allows for genuine psychological introspection. Life's fundamental questions reduce to binary choices, which connects, somehow, to digital principles.

Alter 1 argues that couples should minimize verbal communication, replacing it with physical gestures and intimacy, while reserving language for the outside world. The reasoning: vocabulary differences between two people generate most of the misunderstanding. Cut out the medium, cut out the noise.

**Alter 2** points out that people have different "physical vocabularies" too.

The conversation becomes self-referential. They discuss why dialogue always degenerates, blaming the mechanics of typed text. They banter about women and knowledge. Eventually Alter 2 walks out.

Alter 1 is left where it started.
