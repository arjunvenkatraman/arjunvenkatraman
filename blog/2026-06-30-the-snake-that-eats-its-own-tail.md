A student came to the master holding an old phone. "Master, the snake grows longer with every dot it eats. But the moment it touches itself, the game ends. Why must the thing that feeds it be the thing that finishes it?"
The master watched the snake turn. "What is the snake made of?"
"Pixels."
"Before it ate the first dot, how long was it?"
"One square."
"And now?"
"Forty."
"Then thirty-nine of these squares were dots on the board a moment ago. The snake does not eat food. The snake is its food, set in a line, with a hunger at the front." The master moved a thumb. "The tail you fear is only the oldest meal, still travelling."
The student watched the head chase the empty board. "Then when the head reaches the tail —"
"— it does not collide. It catches up. The eater arrives at the eaten and finds they were never two."

The student bowed and left.
