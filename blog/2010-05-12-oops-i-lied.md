"Don't make me a liar."

I used to find that phrase puzzling. Logically it seemed like a contradiction — how could another person's actions determine whether I'm a liar? I lied or I didn't.

Then I understood it as a request and an opportunity for enrollment into what the speaker is creating. Not an accusation. An invitation. A chance to partner with them and fulfill their vision.

Two perspectives: as someone committed to service, it's an opportunity to help. As someone mastering creation, it's validation of collaborative ability.

Changing your perspective can transform seemingly absurd statements into meaningful opportunities for connection and shared creation.
