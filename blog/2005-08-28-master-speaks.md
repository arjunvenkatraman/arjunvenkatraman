Machines should become extensions of the programmer's self, with code functioning as the connecting element. Keystrokes and heartbeats. Neural impulses.

He who can Design, but not Code is like one who can see the Cup but is unable to lift it to his lips. He who can Code but not Design is blind. True mastery requires both.

---

**The Parable of Two Students**

A master assigns two students to merge ascending and descending sort functions.

The first student mechanically combines the code. The master rejects him.

The second admits failure: the functions seem indivisible, so he has bound them together with conceptual integrity. He is accepted as a pupil.

---

Understanding objects by their function and purpose, rather than their internal components, represents readiness. True mastery means grasping the indivisibility of an object.
