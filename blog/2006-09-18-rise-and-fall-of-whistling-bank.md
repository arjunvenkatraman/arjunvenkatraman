Seven years of ICICI Bank, told through five snapshots.

**1999.** Age 16. First visit. Impressed by modern facilities and professional staff — a sharp contrast to typical Indian government banks.

**2002.** Opening a student account in Bangalore. Limited service hours. A manager makes an exception. Positive view reinforced.

**2004.** Lost debit card. Branch understaffed, overcrowded, air conditioning broken. Staff still helpful but visibly stressed.

**2005.** Attempting a bank draft at 7 PM during extended hours. Massive queues. Only four counters open. I abandon the attempt — my bike has been towed from the parking lot.

**2006.** Helping a friend whose bank documents were destroyed in the Mumbai floods. The ICICI Direct office refuses to accept replacement forms without additional signatures from the account holder, who is in the US — citing policy, despite acknowledging the bank's own responsibility for losing the originals.

More to come, I'm sure.
